Listing Diagnosis
⚠️
Wrong buyer audience entirely
Critical
▼
At $824/sq ft, this home was priced 3.6x the New Braunfels average of $227/sq ft. That price is only justifiable to a buyer who understands riverfront scarcity, STR income potential, and Hill Country lifestyle value. A standard MLS listing reaches mostly conventional homebuyers who see an 880 sq ft home at $725K and move on. The listing needed to be marketed directly to STR investors, second-home buyers, and vacation property seekers — a completely different channel.
📋
STR income data was never presented
Critical
▼
The listing description mentions "also a short term rental" almost as an afterthought. For an investor buyer, the most important data points are annual gross revenue, occupancy rate, average nightly rate, and net income after expenses. None of this was in the listing. A buyer who can't model the return won't make an offer — they'll just move on to a listing that shows the numbers.
📸
Marketing didn't match the price point
High impact
▼
A $725,000 riverfront property with STR income demands professional photography, aerial drone footage of the river access, a virtual tour (which the MLS notes did exist but wasn't promoted effectively), and lifestyle content showing the property in use — tubing launches, evening patios, the Guadalupe at golden hour. At this price, buyers are making an emotional and financial decision simultaneously. The marketing needs to sell the lifestyle first and the numbers second.
🏗️
Showing logistics worked against it
Medium impact
▼
Active STR properties are notoriously difficult to show — guests book weekends, the best times to view are when it's occupied. The agent remarks confirm this ("be sure to make an appointment"). For out-of-town investor buyers — who are your most likely purchaser — friction in the showing process kills deals. A dedicated showing strategy, video walkthroughs, and flexible access options are essential for STR properties.
🌊

Opportunity
